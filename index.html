About Me

I’m a Grade 11 student from Winnipeg, Manitoba, balancing the unique challenges of dual enrollment. Education has always been a priority for me, but I also believe in exploring opportunities outside the classroom to grow as a person. That’s why I’ve dedicated my time to leadership roles, volunteering, and projects that help me make an impact in my community.

One of my proudest achievements is mentoring Grade 6 kids at CanU. It’s not just about teaching—it’s about connecting with younger students and helping them see their own potential. Volunteering at the library has also been a rewarding experience, where I get to interact with people of all ages and contribute to something as timeless as the love for reading and learning. These experiences have taught me the value of giving back and building meaningful relationships.

When I’m not studying or volunteering, you’ll often find me immersed in creative work. Creativity helps me express myself and stay grounded. On a different note, I enjoy esports, which is a fun way to unwind and challenge myself competitively with friends.

Looking ahead, I’m working towards becoming a data scientist—a field that combines my love for problem-solving and technology. I’m particularly interested in using AI to create meaningful solutions, like my work on the BrightPath app, which focuses on mental health. My goal is to continue pushing my limits and exploring how I can use my skills to create positive change, whether it’s through tech innovation, leadership, or creative storytelling.

Whether it’s mentoring kids at CanU, writing a story that resonates with someone, or developing an AI solution that changes lives, I want every step I take to bring me closer to building a better future—not just for myself, but for the people around me.
